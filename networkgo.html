Vehicle Hire Network launches new partnership with Global Go!

Vehicle Hire Network has partnered with Global Go! to create Network Go!, a new broker platform with a focus on connecting with independent rental companies.

The new initiative provides rental companies with a simple, flexible,and risk-free way to expandtheir client base and receive more bookings.

When the pandemic hit, many independent rental companies were left in a place of uncertainty. Network Go! aims to support these businesses by directing the nationwide demand for rental vehicles into the handsof independent suppliers.

By becoming a registered partner, suppliers join a network of operators with a common passion for the industry and for providing a quality service to clients.

When a client places a booking via Network Go!, an initial call will be made to the rental supplier. There is no obligation for the rental company to accept the booking and no commission fee. The platform operates using a standardised tariff.

The partnership exists to direct more business to independent suppliers that are so often overlooked in favour of national and international corporates.

We caught up with Network Go! Development Manager, Denise House to find out more:

What is the aim of the Network Go! partnership?

The overall aim of Network Go! is to expandour coverage and direct more business to independent rental providers. Many of our clients require nationwide coverage. Network Go! allows us to provide that coverage across the UK and Ireland whilst supporting independents, operators that are often overlooked by brokers in favour of national corporates.

How does it work?

It’s a very simple process. If a rental company wants to come on board with us, they agree the rate, sign our terms and conditions, and then it's down to us to provide customers and business for them. The rental company can focus on what they do best, the rest is down to us.

Is there any long term commitment?

No, it's all very flexible. The rental company decides if they want to accept a booking and they're not tied into any long-termcontract. Once they havesigned up, we apply for an account and use them as often as we can.

Do operators pay commission on bookings received via Network GO!?

No, we have a standard buy-in tariff which makes it fair. We're not trying to undercut rental companies and make huge margins, that's not what this project is about.

Can you tell us more about the booking process?

It's very simple. A client approaches us with a booking request, we then search for the vehicle using Vehicle Hire Network and call the partner to confirm if they are happy to accept the booking. It's a partnership where we respect each other, if a rental company doesn't want to accept a booking, we'll respect that.

Why was there a focus on serving the needs of corporate clients?

There are a lot of corporate blue-chip companies out there using platforms that still require a lot of manual paperwork and scanning. Network Go! provides a completely digital alternative. Opening an account is done online, agreeing damage is online, checking fleet maintenance is all online. It's giving them a choice.

Does the rental company incur any risk?

No, from the rental company’s point of view, it's risk-free. We’re the ones who are looking after the customers, we’re the ones who are taking the bookings. Rental companies don't take the risk because the account sits with Global. If a client failed to pay for a booking, therental company would still get paid.

Can anyone join Network Go!?

To join Network GO!, rental operators need to be a Vehicle Hire Network member and use Prohire Companion. Use of our Estim-8 system is also a requirement of the partnership and providedfree of charge.

Working with the Vehicle Hire Network is essential for us. If a client wants a vehicle, they want it straight away and Vehicle Hire Network makes it quick and easy for us to locate vehicles in a particular location.

Prohire Companion is a requirement because, in rental, one of the key problems is customers not agreeing to damage or paying for it. Some rental companies are still using manual check-sheets, ticking boxes and drawing things. We want the whole process to be seamless, completely paperless and Prohire Companion makes that possible.

Working with operators that use Companion keeps things simple, everything is agreed upfront, preventing disputes down the line.
