Vehicle Hire Network launches new partnership with Global Go!

Vehicle Hire Network has partnered with Global Go! to create Network Go!, a new platform designed to support independent rental companies.

The new partnership provides Prohire users with a simple, flexible way to expand their existing client base and receive risk-free bookings.

The COVID-19 pandemic left many independent rental companies in a place of uncertainty. Network Go! aims to support these businesses by directing the nationwide demand for rental vehicles into the hands of independent suppliers.

By becoming a Network Go! partner, rental operators join a network of suppliers with a shared passion for the industry and a desire to deliver quality service.

The partnership exists to direct business to independent suppliers, often overlooked in favour of national and international corporates. There is no obligation to accept a booking, no long-term commitment, and no commission fee.

We caught up with Network Go! Development Manager, Denise House, to find out more.

Fay Austen: What is the aim of the new partnership?

Denise House: The overall aim is to expand our coverage and direct more business to independent suppliers. Many of our clients require nationwide coverage; Network Go! allows us to provide that coverage across the UK and Ireland whilst supporting independent operators.

FA: How does it work?

DH: It's a very straightforward process. If a rental company wants to come on board, they agree the rate, sign the terms and conditions, and then it's down to us to provide them with bookings. The rental company can focus on what they do best; the rest is down to us.

FA: Is there any long-term commitment?

DH: No, it's all very flexible. The rental company decides if they want to accept a booking and there's no long-term contract. Once they have signed up, we apply for an account and use them as often as we can.

FA: Do operators pay commission on bookings received?

DH: No, we have a standard buy-in tariff which makes things fair. We're not trying to undercut rental companies and make huge margins, that's not the aim of the partnership.

FA: Can you tell us more about the booking process?

DH: It's very straightforward. A client approaches us with a booking request; we search for the vehicle using Vehicle Hire Network and call the supplier to confirm the booking. It's a partnership where we respect each other, if a rental company doesn't want to accept a booking, we'll respect that.

FA: Why was there a focus on serving the needs of corporate clients?

DH: Many blue-chip corporates use platforms that still require a lot of manual paperwork and scanning. Network Go! provides an entirely digital alternative. It's giving them a choice.

FA: Does the rental company incur any risk?

DH: No, from the rental company's point of view, it's risk-free. Rental companies don't take the risk because the account sits with us. If a client fails to pay for a booking, Network Go! still pay the rental company.

FA: Can anyone join?

DH: Membership is exclusive to Prohire users. Rental operators do also need to be a Vehicle Hire Network member and use Prohire Companion.

Vehicle Hire Network is essential for us. If a client wants a vehicle, they want it straight away. Vehicle Hire Network makes it quick and easy to locate vehicles in a particular location.

Prohire Companion is a requirement because, in rental, one of the main problems is customers not agreeing to damage or paying for it. Some rental companies are still using manual check-sheets, ticking boxes and drawing things. We want the whole process to be seamless, completely paperless, and Prohire Companion makes that possible.

Working with operators that use Prohire Companion keeps things simple, everything is agreed to upfront, preventing disputes down the line.

FA: Is there anything else you would like to add?

DH: This is all about independents doing what they do best. Once again, it's a simple, risk-free way to expand your existing client base and receive more bookings. I would encourage Prohire users to get in touch, and we look forward to forging new relationships as the partnership develops.
